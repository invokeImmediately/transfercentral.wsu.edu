Academic Regulation: 16. MILITARY CREDIT

(Proposed Policy)

Credit will be evaluated on the basis of military transcripts received from active duty military and veterans pursuing an education at any WSU campus.

  1. Transcripted credit:
    1. WSU uses the American Council on Education (ACE) "A Guide to the Evaluation of Educational Experiences in the Armed Services" as a framework for evaluating transferable credit documented on military transcripts.
    2. ACE recommendations for the amount and level of credit (upper or lower division) are followed if the military course is applicable to university or departmental regulations.
  2. DANTES Credit: Credit for DANTES Subject Standardized Tests (DSSTs) will be granted for college-level academic subjects (non-vocational/technical courses) using the minimum score and credit amount recommendations of the American Council on Education.

Lower-division transfer credit is limited to 73 credits from all sources (transfer, credit by exam, military, other). Total transferable credit is limited to 90 credits.

Admissions, Transfer, and Veterans Websites/Letter to Veterans:

At WSU, we use the following procedures to ensure every veteran receives the maximum amount of college credit for military training possible.

  1. WSU evaluates every military transcript received from active duty military and veterans pursuing an education here.
  2. WSU uses the American Council on Education (ACE) "A Guide to the Evaluation of Educational Experiences in the Armed Services" as a guide. ACE translates military courses and occupations into academic credit recommendations, and provides guidelines to interpret and recommend credit for college courses.
  3. ACE recommendations for the amount and level of credit (upper or lower division) are followed if the military course is applicable to university or departmental regulations.
  4. ACE credits on transcripts that do not match any courses in any degree of pursuit may be accepted as elective credits if applicable to the student’s degree program.
  5. Lower-division transfer credit is limited to 73 credits from all sources (transfer, credit by exam, military, other). Total transferable credit is limited to 90 credits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can military credit transcripts be accessed?

The Joint Services Transcript for Army, Coast Guard, Marine Corps, and Navy active duty, reserve, and veterans can be requested at jst.doded.mil/smart.

Community College of the Air Force transcripts can be requested at www.au.af.mil/au/barnes/ccaf.

Please Note: Military transcripts may also list College Level Test Scores such as DANTES Subject Standardized Tests (DSST) or Defense Language Proficiency Test (DLPT), and credit can be awarded based on the American Council of Education (ACE) recommendation. College Level Examination Program (CLEP) scores may also be evaluated off these transcripts.
