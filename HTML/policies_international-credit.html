Evaluation of International Transcripts

Transcripts from institutions outside the U.S. are evaluated by the Office of International Programs. International Programs has developed a team of credential evaluators who evaluate international transcripts  International transcripts are evaluated by the Office of International Programs with assistance of departmental contacts for specific equivalencies. Please contact Geraldine Scholz with questions about the process.

The process of international credit evaluation can also be found by referring to the Transcripts section of the International Admission Requirements webpage.

Evaluation and Translation:

  • Freshman students who do not have college or university coursework need to request that the evaluation include a cumulative grade point average (GPA).
  • If you have less than one year of college/university work, you will need to have high school/secondary and college/university transcripts evaluated.
  • Transfer students who have at least one year of college/university credit need to request a course-by-course detailed evaluation of their college transcripts. (If you have at least 23 semester credits of transferable college work, you do not need to provide secondary-school transcripts.)

Transcript evaluation can be done by any of the organizations with membership in NACES (the National Association of Credential Evaluation Services). You may want to start with one of these commonly used services:

  • World Education Service
  • International Education Research Foundation

Please Note: According to Academic Regulation 6.(j), an international degree will be evaluated on a course-by-course basis for fulfillment of requirements, in the absence of an approved articulation.  Higher degrees do not necessarily fulfill baccalaureate degree requirements.
