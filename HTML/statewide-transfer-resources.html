WSU subscribes to the “Policy on Intercollege Transfer and Articulation Among Washington Public Colleges and Universities” endorsed by the public colleges and universities of Washington and the State Board for Community and Technical Colleges and published by the Student Achievement Council.

The information and resources below will provide more context and information about various statewide transfer policies and procedures.

The Washington Council for High School-College Relations

The WCHSCR operates for educational purposes, and is organized to assist all Washington state students to access and engage in post-secondary opportunities at its member institutions.

State Board for Community and Technical Colleges

The SBCTC is governed by a nine-member Governor-appointed board responsible for administering the Community and Technical College Act and providing leadership and coordination for Washington's system of 34 public community and technical colleges.

  • Direct Transfer Associate's Degrees The DTA degree designed to transfer to most bachelor's programs in the state and major related program pathways.
  • Associate in Science-Transfer (AS-T) Designed to meet prerequisites for most majors in engineering and science.
  • Associate in Applied Science-Transfer (AAS-T) Focuses on technical courses for job preparation, but also includes some college-level general education courses.
  • Transfer Resources Information regarding degree approval, MRPs, Policies &Agreements, Publications, Reports, Organization, and Transfer Tools.
  • Transfer Degree Inventory A spreadsheet showing all degrees other than applied offered by Washington CTCs.
  • Common Course Numbering Simplifies transfer process with commonly shared courses with the same course numbers with an "&" among Washingtong CTCs.
  • Reverse Transfer Articulation  If you earned 60 quarter credits or 40 semester credits, you’re eligible to earn your associate degree even after you transferred
  • High School/College Programs Information on programs for high school students earning college credits: College in the High School (CHS), Running Start, and Tech Prep.
  • Prior Learning Assessment of Credit Guidelines for assessment methods, awarding credit, and the processes involved.
  • Joint Transfer Council The council that considers statewide transfer issues and recommends policy strategies.
  • The Washington 45 A list of courses designed to transfer toward general education requirements at any other public and most private institutions in the state.
